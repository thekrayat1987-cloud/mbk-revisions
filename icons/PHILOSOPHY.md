# Dimensional Candy

**Philosophy**: Friendly sculpture in pixel form — icons as tiny pieces of rendered jewelry.

## Visual Movement

Every icon is a small sculpted object, not a flat symbol. It carries weight, catches light, and casts soft shadow. The aesthetic sits at the crossroads of Fluent 3D, clay-render playfulness, and the calm precision of Pixar props — rounded, glossy, chromatic, but never childish. Each shape is meticulously crafted to feel touchable: a pressable candy, a just-unwrapped gem, a small object you want to hold.

## Form and Light

Volume communicates through gradient transitions, not through explicit geometry. A single diagonal light source from the upper-left creates a consistent specular highlight across all eight pieces — a thin arc of near-white on the top edge, a shadow pooling underneath at 60% opacity with a 12px soft blur. Surfaces curve. Nothing is hard-edged, nothing is drawn in outline. The work of countless subtle refinements: a 2px highlight rim that separates each form from its background, a second darker rim inside to imply thickness.

## Color and Material

Color comes in pairs — a vivid hue meeting a deeper version of itself along a 135° gradient. The palette is high-saturation but anchored by restraint: no neons, no muddy mid-tones. Each icon owns its hue completely, but all eight share the same material quality — a soft plasticine-glass hybrid that feels expensive. Highlights are cool white with a trace of the base hue; shadows are a desaturated indigo rather than black, so nothing falls into darkness.

## Scale and Rhythm

Each icon occupies the same visual weight inside its 256×256 frame. ~15% padding on all sides. The silhouette sits centered, not floating toward a corner. Every icon renders at the same optical size — this is the product of painstaking attention, a master craftsman balancing a bell against a book against a bolt of lightning so all three feel equally substantial.

## Composition

Compositions are single-subject and centered, with subtle depth — a card stack fans three deep, a book opens at 30°, a speech bubble overlaps a second at 20% offset. Depth without clutter. The result reads instantly at 52×52 on mobile yet rewards close inspection at 200% zoom, where every gradient stop and rim-light reveals itself. Master-level execution: the kind of detail that only registers subconsciously, but makes the difference between a UI that feels generic and one that feels cared-for.

## Discipline

No decoration for its own sake. No sparkles, no stars, no extra garnish. If an element isn't carrying information — the subject, the light, the shadow — it doesn't belong. Eight icons, eight hues, one material. The consistency itself is the craft.
